Changing your air filter

A clogged filter makes your system work harder.

  • Change every 90 days
  • With pets, every 30–60 days
  • Use MERV 8–11 rated filters
▶ Watch: How to change your AC air filter (3 min)
Step-by-step
  • 1
    Turn off your HVAC system at the thermostat before starting.
  • 2
    Locate the filter slot — usually near the return air duct or inside the air handler.
  • 3
    Slide the old filter out and check the size printed on the frame.
  • 4
    Insert the new filter with the airflow arrow pointing toward the unit.
  • 5
    Turn your system back on and set a reminder for 90 days from today.

Setting your thermostat

The right setting makes a big difference in comfort and cost.

  • Set to 78°F when home
  • Set to 82°F when away
  • Avoid below 72°F
▶ Watch: Best thermostat settings for Florida summers (2 min)
Step-by-step
  • 1
    Open your thermostat app or panel and select "Home" mode.
  • 2
    Set the temperature to 78°F for daytime comfort.
  • 3
    Create an "Away" schedule set to 82°F for when you leave.
  • 4
    Save the schedule so it runs automatically every day.

Clearing your outdoor unit

Your condenser needs airflow to release heat.

  • Keep 2 feet of clearance
  • Remove debris monthly
▶ Watch: How to clean your outdoor condenser unit (4 min)
Step-by-step
  • 1
    Turn off power to the unit at the breaker before touching it.
  • 2
    Clear leaves, grass clippings, and debris from around the base.
  • 3
    Trim back any plants or shrubs within 2 feet of the unit.
  • 4
    Gently rinse the fins with a garden hose, spraying from the inside out.
  • 5
    Let it dry fully before restoring power.

Annual tune-up

Book March–April to beat the summer rush.

  • Checks refrigerant, electrical, drain lines
  • Extends system life 3–5 years

What we'll check during your visit

    Refrigerant levels checked and topped off if needed.
  • 2
    Electrical connections inspected for wear.
  • 3
    Condensate drain line cleared to prevent leaks.
  • 4
    Coils cleaned for maximum efficiency.
